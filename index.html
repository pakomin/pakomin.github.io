Thank you by Dido is a beautiful song. I find it comforting and nice and for once I don't mind publicly professing love for a mainstream pop song. Her voice is soft and unique and with it, she sings about the comfort she finds in being around a particular person. Her life is otherwise difficult and the weather is grey, but it's okay because she has this person's picture on her wall. she's running late for work and all the coworkers will imply that she'd get fired soon, but then she gets a call from the person and it all feels not that bad after all. She then sings "thank you" to them for giving her the best day of her life. It's really sweet. I can somewhat relate to it ~ having known someone who I miss and who made a some of my days meaningful and precious which otherwise would have been bad. Somtimes I want to sing this song out of love and care for her (the person from my past, not Dido (or maybe even Dido, for making this song and thereby helping me recognize this feeling of gratitude and expressing it here)).

The song is comforting to me in other ways too - instrumentally for having a melodious flute, gentle guitar strumming, and relaxed percussions. It has the soft musical aesthetic of a 90s track. I don't remember how i discovered it but it has grown on me ever since.

Its music video I find perplexing sometimes. It shows Dido in a pretty house, looking into the camera calmly and singing, while there's police at her doorstep who have arrived to evict her. They then break into her house to repossess everything. All this while she sings about this person in her life who has brought meaning and goodness to it. She's really calm through it all too - looking into the camera with an expression that i cannot tell if is more sad or grateful. Maybe both? Maybe it is was intended to be ambiguous. It is confusing however to see her remain composed and sing about a loved one while getting removed from her beautiful home. Is this okay? Is this a form of love that's reasonable and healthy? During my previous (and only) relationship I believed in the importance of it being a positive force and influence in my life. It felt important to remember to take care of our individual respective selves and continue to nurture the part of our lives that existed outside the relationship too . Become blinded by love to the ponit of seeing it turn to our detriment would color the relationship in shades of gray - introducing mixed feelings to something that felt so vibrant, beautiful, and life-affirming otherwise. I wonder if that's how Dido feels as her beautiful house is getting taken apart from around her, as the flowers in her garden get trampled by a bulldozer, and she's forcibly taken out to the street. The closest thing she sings about the situation occurring in the video is:

"And even if my house falls down now,
I wouldn't have a clue,
Because you're near me.."

Wouldn't it be heart-rending to see one's house fall down? To not even be able to even recognize it from happening because one is so taken by someone else? Or would it be a very touching and beautiful thing?; To be part of a love so affirming and nice that such a travesty doesn't even register as such? I don't know. When I first saw the video i wondered if it was secretly about the ability of love to blind and bring about one's own downfall - and I thought this while I was actually doing well in life and in a joyous relationship. However, these days I believe that Dido may have meant it sincerely, that the video is intended without irony at all. I say this while currently doing terribly in life, having had a downfall of the sort, and while being not in that relationship anymore. hah. The tipsy-turvy nature of things. It would make more sense if my perception of the video was more aligned by my personal circumstances at the time of seeing it, but they're somehow the exact opposite.

The next few lyrics in the song are:

"And I want to thank you
For giving me the best day of my life
Oh, just to be with you,
is having the best day of my life"

As I hear her voice sing this part, I believe I truly understand what she means because I've felt this way about this person from my past, and genuinely feel grateful and glad that I encountered her. I had some of the best days of my life because of her being in them. The video however shows Dido's house getting bulldozed and destroyed. It seems that she is having the worst day of her life while singing about the best.

Considering the beautiful and meaningful relationship that comes to my mind and the painful way in which it ended resulting in my devastation in more than one way, I've come to lean more towards the theory that she's singing sincerely. That even as her house has been torn down, she feels grateful and glad for her partner's presence in her life. The partner that is nowhere to be seen near her in the video as something really unfortunate is happening to her.

Until recently, I had just assumed that the person being sung about is a past lover. But it could just as well be a friend, or a parent, or one's child even. None of what Dido says is necessarily romantic. To deduce who it is about, I tried to look for clues in the video. It dawned on me that that she is looking directly into the camera, at .. me(?). Is the person she's referring to the whole time me in particular? Surely not. It's unlikely that Dido knows me, an ignominous nobody on the other side of the planet, and I've never been around her (from what i recall), much less give her a great day in her life. Maybe it is about the people who listen to her music and watch her music videos. But that would run counter to her references to the picture on her wall, her getting the call on the way to work, and her getting handed the towel as she returns from outside soaking wet due to the rain. It has to be someone more personal than the general person who listens to her music. So I guess we're back to a lover or a partner being the most likely person in question here. A more cynical view would be that she's just singing because she knows how to sing and that it is easy to write a song about love and profitable to do so because we all experience it in one form or another. It is a universal theme and the nature of love remains elusive, large, and unencapsulable to the point that it can be endlessly drawn upon for creative inspiration. I'm not sure. When I checked wikipedia to read about the background of this song, I was glad to see that it had indeed been written wrt someone she knew; her boyfriend at the time.

I hope I can some day write about my own experiences of love. Meanwhile, this text is my version of saying thank you to Dido for writing "Thank you" the song, to help me connect with how i feel about my past partner to whom i'd like to have said "thank you" for having been in my life.
